THE BRAIN
Welcome to the Interactive Cerebral Cortex!
The cerebral cortex is the outermost section of the brain that appears to have several folds and wrinkles. The cortex is divided into two hemispheres, the left and right side of the brain. These hemispheres are connected by the structure called the corpus callosum (to learn more about this structure, click on it in the diagram below). Each hemisphere consists of four lobes: frontal, temporal, parietal and occipital.

Each of these lobes has a specific set of functions, however, the lobes work together to make us who we are! Click on each lobe to learn more about its function.
Welcome to the Interactive Sagittal Plane!
The interior of the brain and its structures can be viewed in three main types of planes: coronal, horizontal and sagittal. The most convenient way to examine the structures of the brain is in a sagittal plane. The sagittal plane separates the brain into the right and left hemispheres.

Each of the structures seen on the left has its own functions but they work together to carry out tasks. Click on the various structures to understand more about its function.

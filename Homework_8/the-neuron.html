Welcome to the Interactive Neuron!
Neurons are cells within the nervous system that transmit and receive information from other neurons, muscle cells and glands. Neurons transmit information through the generation of electrical signals called action potentials, which we will go over on the next page.

There are four main categories of neurons: unipolar, bipolar, pseudounipolar, and multipolar. The neuron we are currently looking at is a multipolar neuron.

Like the other three types of neurons, a multipolar neuron consists of one axon. However, unlike the others it contains many dendritic branches that allow it to integrate information from several other cells.
